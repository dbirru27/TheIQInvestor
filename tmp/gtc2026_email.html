Here's a comprehensive summary of Jensen Huang's keynote at NVIDIA GTC 2026, delivered on March 16 at the SAP Center in San Jose to a capacity crowd.

🔑 Overarching Theme: The Age of the Token

Huang framed "the token" as the fundamental unit of modern AI — the building block behind scientific discovery, virtual worlds, and autonomous machines. He celebrated CUDA's 20th anniversary, calling it the "flywheel" driving accelerated computing across every phase of the AI lifecycle. NVIDIA's GPU computing demand, he said, has grown 1 million times in recent years, leading him to forecast at least $1 trillion in cumulative revenue from 2025–2027.

🖥️ Hardware Roadmap: Vera Rubin & Feynman

Vera Rubin Platform — now in full production — is NVIDIA's new full-stack computing platform purpose-built for agentic AI. It comprises 7 chips, 5 rack-scale systems, and 1 supercomputer, including the new Vera CPU (2× efficiency, 50% faster than traditional rack-scale CPUs) and BlueField-4 STX storage architecture. Huang emphasized "extreme codesign" — software and silicon designed in tandem.

Looking further ahead, Huang unveiled the Feynman architecture featuring a new CPU called "Rosa" (named after Rosalind Franklin). Feynman pairs the LP40 next-gen LPU with BlueField-5 and CX10, connected via NVIDIA Kyber (copper + co-packaged optics). It advances every pillar: compute, memory, storage, networking, and security.

🚀 AI Infrastructure: From Data Centers to Orbit

NVIDIA released the Vera Rubin DSX AI Factory reference design and an Omniverse DSX Blueprint, allowing companies to simulate AI factories digitally before physical deployment. In a bold announcement, Huang revealed NVIDIA is extending its computing platform into space — "Space-1 Vera Rubin" will power orbital data centers, bringing AI compute to geospatial intelligence and autonomous space operations.

He also introduced DLSS 5 — 3D-guided neural rendering enabling real-time, photoreal 4K performance on local hardware — continuing NVIDIA's GeForce legacy as "the house that GeForce built."

🤖 Open-Source & Agentic AI: OpenClaw & NemoClaw

Huang spotlighted OpenClaw, calling it "the most popular open-source project in the history of humanity." NVIDIA is announcing full platform support for OpenClaw, enabling developers to stand up AI agents with a single command. He declared: "Every single company in the world today has to have an OpenClaw strategy."

For enterprise-grade deployment, he introduced the NVIDIA OpenShell runtime and the NemoClaw stack — combining policy enforcement, network guardrails, and privacy routing to serve as "the policy engine of all the SaaS companies in the world."

NVIDIA also launched the Nemotron Coalition — six frontier model families built with global AI lab partners:

  1. Nemotron — Language & reasoning
  2. Cosmos — World & vision
  3. Isaac GR00T — General-purpose robotics
  4. Alpaymayo — Autonomous driving
  5. BioNeMo — Biology & chemistry
  6. Earth-2 — Weather & climate

🏭 Physical AI & Robotics

The physical AI ecosystem is expanding rapidly. New automaker partners include BYD, Hyundai, Nissan, and Geely, with an Uber partnership for robotaxi deployment. Robotics collaborations span ABB, Universal Robots, and KUKA, while T-Mobile is converting base stations into edge AI platforms.

In a memorable closing, a walking Olaf robot (Disney's Frozen) took the stage — powered by NVIDIA's physical AI stack, Newton physics engine, and Omniverse simulation — followed by singing robots, a digital Jensen avatar, and an animated lobster performing a campfire song.

📊 Key Takeaways for Business

  • $1T+ revenue forecast (2025–2027) signals unprecedented demand for AI infrastructure
  • Vera Rubin in full production — enterprises can begin planning deployments now
  • Agentic AI is the new paradigm — OpenClaw/NemoClaw creates the enterprise agent stack
  • Physical AI is crossing the chasm — major OEMs and telcos are on board
  • Space computing opens an entirely new frontier for AI workloads
